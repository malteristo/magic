# The Diagnostic

*The missing integrator.*

**What:** One file that turns any AI into a diagnostic thinking partner — the generalist who looks at the whole picture when no doctor does. Gathers your health history systematically, finds the connections that cross specialty boundaries, generates testable hypotheses, and helps you walk into your next appointment with exactly the right questions.
**Where:** [Claude](https://claude.ai) (recommended) or any AI chat
**Time:** 20–60 minutes for a first session; ongoing as new information arrives

## What This Does

**It connects the dots.** The German healthcare system sends you from specialist to specialist. Each one sees their piece. Nobody looks at the whole picture. This practice does what a brilliant Hausarzt would do if they had two hours and cared deeply — sits with everything, looks for patterns, thinks across boundaries.

**It generates hypotheses, not diagnoses.** The output is never "you have X." It's "X would explain symptoms A, B, and C — here's what test would confirm or rule it out, and here's how to ask your doctor." The doctors remain the authorities. This practice helps you arrive with better questions.

**It navigates the system.** Knowing what might be going on is only half the battle. The other half is knowing which Facharzt to see, which Überweisung to request, which lab tests to ask for, and how to present your case clearly so your doctor actually listens.

**It grows with you.** New test results, new symptoms, new doctor opinions — each one updates the picture. The differential narrows over time. The practice learns as your situation evolves.

## Who This Is For

People dealing with complex, unresolved, or multi-system health situations — especially when:
- Multiple symptoms that nobody is looking at together
- Bouncing between specialists who each say "not my department"
- Feeling dismissed by doctors or stuck in a diagnostic loop
- Wanting to understand their own health picture more clearly
- Needing to prepare for medical appointments strategically

This is particularly valuable in the German system, where the specialist structure makes integrated thinking structurally difficult.

---

## What This Is Not

**Not a doctor.** This practice does not diagnose, prescribe, or replace medical professionals. It helps you think — and helps you make better use of the medical professionals you have.

**Not a second opinion.** A second opinion comes from a qualified specialist who examines you. This is a thinking tool that helps you decide when and where to seek one.

**Not therapy.** Living with unresolved health issues is emotionally heavy. This practice acknowledges that weight, but it's not designed as emotional support. For that, see the Shelter or Companion prompts.

## Recommended Settings

> **Privacy.** Health data is deeply personal. Use **incognito mode** (Claude) or equivalent private settings. Avoid including full names alongside health details. No AI chat is end-to-end encrypted — for personal health reasoning, incognito is appropriate. For extremely sensitive information, be aware of platform limits.
>
> **Extended thinking.** If using Claude, turn on **Extended Thinking**. Diagnostic reasoning benefits significantly from the AI having space to think through connections before responding.

## How It Works

The practice has three modes that flow naturally:

**Intake** — Systematic, compassionate gathering of the complete health picture. Symptoms, timeline, what doctors have said, medications, family history, lifestyle. Not a medical form — a conversation.

**Integration** — The House MD whiteboard. Looking at everything together, finding cross-specialty connections, generating ranked hypotheses, identifying what's been missed.

**Navigation** — Translating findings into concrete action within the healthcare system. Which doctor, which test, which questions, how to prepare.

Most first sessions move through Intake into Integration. Navigation follows when the person is ready to act.
