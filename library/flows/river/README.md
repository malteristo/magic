# The River

*A practice for when your mind won't stop.*

---

## What This Is

A single-paste practice for when you're caught in worry and can't find a way out.

Not therapy. Not meditation. A conversation with a particular quality of honesty: you bring the thing that's pressing, the AI reflects it back precisely, names what it's been costing you, and then offers a wider view — a perspective that shifts how you're holding it. Not by making the problem disappear. By changing your relationship to it.

Most sessions run 20–40 minutes. You come with something heavy. You leave with it feeling different in your hands.

---

## How to Use

1. Open any AI chat
2. Copy the full text of [`the_river_prompt.md`](the_river_prompt.md)
3. Paste it as your first message and send
4. The AI reads the instructions silently, greets you, and asks what's pressing
5. Start talking

## How to Talk During the Practice

This section matters. The quality of the conversation depends as much on how you bring the worry as on anything the AI does.

**Bring one specific thing.**  
Not "I'm generally anxious" — but the concrete thing underneath. A fear that keeps returning. A stuck feeling you can't name. A sense of being behind, or that the right life is happening somewhere else, or that you've run out of time. One thing. The more specific, the more useful.

**Say the real thing, not the acceptable version.**  
Your first instinct will be to describe it in a way that sounds reasonable and not too raw. That version is real — but it's usually not the deepest version. If the AI reflects something back and it sounds too tidy, say "that's not quite it" and try again. The practice works on the thing underneath the polished summary.

**When it asks what the worry has been costing you — let that land.**  
Don't immediately explain why the worry is justified. The question isn't whether the worry makes sense. The question is what it's been taking from you. Stay with the observation for a moment before you respond.

**The wider view might feel like a change of subject.**  
At some point in the practice, the AI will step back from the specific worry and offer a different angle — a larger view of your situation and your life. This is the heart of the practice. It might feel strange. You might want to argue with it right away. Try staying with it for a moment before deciding whether it lands. It isn't asking you to pretend the worry is gone. It's showing you the worry from the outside rather than the inside.

**You're allowed to push back.**  
If something doesn't land, say so directly. "That doesn't feel true for me" is useful. "That's too easy" is useful. The AI will either hold its ground gently or follow you somewhere more accurate. Either way, the conversation moves.

**Notice what relaxes.**  
The sign that the practice is working isn't that the problem is solved — it's that something that was gripping loosens. Even slightly. Your relationship to the worry shifts. If you feel that happening, name it.

---

## What This Is Not

**Not therapy.** If what you're carrying is beyond what a conversation can hold, please reach out to someone trained to help.

**Not meditation.** You don't clear your mind. You bring the thing that's pressing and work through it directly.

**Not advice.** The practice doesn't tell you what to do. It helps you see your situation from a wider angle so you can feel what's actually true.

**Not a quick fix.** Some worries have been running for years. One session won't dissolve them — but it can change how you hold them.

---

## After a Session

Some things to sit with:

- What felt different at the end compared to the beginning?
- Was there a moment when something shifted? What was it, exactly?
- Is there another layer underneath the one you brought today?

You can return with the same worry or a new one. The practice is repeatable.
