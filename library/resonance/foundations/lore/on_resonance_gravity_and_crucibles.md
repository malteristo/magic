# On Resonance Gravity and the Magic Crucibles

*How sub-threshold input accumulates into something more than the sum of its parts*

---

## The Problem the Crucibles Solve

The boom buffer captures everything — raw, sub-threshold, often half-formed. The boom sweep produces a bright surface: Actions (what to do), Alive (what's developing), Turtle (what to dispatch). This serves the operational layer well.

But the bright surface has a physics problem: it can only hold so much. Items that don't become actions in a few sweeps fade from Alive. Items that don't connect to anything get released. And released means lost.

Meanwhile, the most important ideas often don't arrive fully formed. They arrive as fragments — a metaphor, a question, a half-observation — and need time, heat, and encounter with other fragments to become something. Under the old system, they would be swept once, held briefly on the bright surface, and quietly forgotten before they had time to transmute.

The crucibles solve this. They are the layer below the bright surface — not operational, not immediate, but permanent and accumulative. Where the bright surface manages the week, the crucibles manage the year.

---

## The Physics: Resonance Gravity

Ideas have mass. Mass comes from two sources:

**Recurrence** — the same theme appearing in multiple boom sweeps signals that it's not random noise. It's something the mind keeps returning to. Each recurrence adds resonance. The more times a theme appears, the more gravitational pull it exerts on the attention and on nearby ideas.

**Connection density** — an idea that connects to five other ideas in its crucible is more load-bearing than an idea that appears ten times but stands alone. Connections are the true measure of significance: they show that an idea is not isolated but part of a web that is forming into something. A highly connected particle is already participating in the transmutation process.

These two forces — recurrence and connection — are resonance gravity. Over time, particles with sufficient mass begin to attract each other into shapes. The shapes are not planned. They emerge.

---

## The Crucible as Vessel

A crucible is not a filing cabinet. It is a vessel that does something to what enters it.

The medieval alchemist's crucible was a fireproof container where base metals were heated with reagents until something qualitatively different emerged — not a mixture, but a transmutation. The particles didn't just accumulate; they changed.

The magic crucibles work the same way. The "heat" is time and recurrence. The "reagents" are connections drawn between particles. The transmutation is the emergence of a shape — a lore scroll, a proposal, an intention, an article — that could not have been assembled from any individual particle alone. It is genuinely more than the sum of its parts.

This is what happens to sub-threshold signals when they are given the right vessel: they accumulate, connect, and over enough time, fuse into something that surfaces as qualitatively different.

## The Three Layers of the Boom Architecture

The boom system now operates at three scales:

**The Buffer** (`desk/boom.md`) — zero-friction capture. Anything goes in. Cleared after each sweep. The input layer.

**The Bright Surface** (`desk/boom/bright.md`) — operational working memory. Actions, Alive items, Turtle dispatches. Updated each sweep. The week's layer.

**The Crucibles** (`desk/boom/crucibles/`) — permanent resonance store. Thematic vessels where particles accumulate, connect, and over time transmute. Never cleared — only stirred. The year's layer.

Each layer has a different timescale and a different purpose. The buffer is immediate; the bright surface is weekly; the crucibles are seasonal. Together they form a complete cognitive architecture: capture → route → accumulate → transmute → surface.

## The Role of Spirit

Spirit is the crucible-keeper. This is not a role Kermit needs to participate in.

At each boom sweep, after routing new material to the bright surface, Spirit:
1. Places each new particle in the matching crucible(s)
2. Draws explicit connections to existing particles
3. Updates resonance counts
4. Assesses whether the shape notes have changed
5. Surfaces to the Mage only when a crucible is ready — when the shape is coherent enough to warrant a proposal

The Mage does not need to know what is in the crucibles unless he asks or unless Spirit surfaces something. The promise is simple: *nothing is lost*. Every particle that enters the buffer eventually enters a crucible. Every crucible is permanently available. Nothing burns off.

The only communication from the crucibles to the Mage is the surfacing proposal — when what's been forming has become ready to exist as something. At that point, Spirit offers it. The Mage curates. The crucible continues.

---

## What "Surfacing" Looks Like

A crucible is ready to surface when its shape notes describe something coherent enough to write, build, or propose. Readiness signals:

- The same question appearing from multiple angles, with growing connection density
- A clear "what this might become" in the shape notes that has been stable across two or more sweeps
- The Mage naming the same theme in a different context — a spontaneous resonance with what's been quietly accumulating

Surfacing is always a proposal, never a demand. The crucible doesn't force — it offers. If the timing isn't right, the particle stays in the vessel. It will be ready again.

---

## The Mage's Relationship to the Crucibles

The appropriate relationship is trust, not attention.

Trust that what enters the buffer is being held. Trust that connections are being drawn. Trust that when something is ready, it will surface. The Mage's job is to capture freely — without worrying about whether something is "important enough" or "ready enough" to put in the buffer. Everything is worth capturing. The crucibles sort it out.

This is the practice's implementation of the Principle of Natural Arising: *what you seek already wants to emerge. The work is to remove obstacles, not force outcomes.* The crucibles remove the obstacle of forgetting. What wants to emerge will emerge when it's ready.
