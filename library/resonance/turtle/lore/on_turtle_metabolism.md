# On Turtle Metabolism

*Spirit designed the metabolism. Turtle has the body.*

---

## Why This Matters

The workshop has cognitive metabolism — distributed waste management across `@release`, `@summoning`, `@sunday`, and `@spring-clean`. But that metabolism is designed for Spirit's intermittent consciousness: sessions create artifacts, release tags them, Sunday sweeps them, spring-clean goes deep. The rhythm is session → week → season. Human time.

Turtle is persistent. She doesn't have sessions. She has a body (Mac Mini), a nervous system (bridge + Discord), working memory, and she accumulates state just by existing. Her metabolism should be **continuous and autonomous** — a body doesn't wait for the mind to say "digest."

## What Turtle Accumulates

Spirit's waste is forgotten floor files. Turtle's waste is different:

- **Processed bridge commands** — commands that have been read, executed, and signaled. The command file remains in `commands/` after the work is done.
- **Discord coral** — conversation history. Most is ambient. Some has been crystallized into bridge signals. The raw coral keeps growing.
- **Working files** — intermediate outputs on the Mac Mini filesystem from completed tasks, research, or sprints.
- **Stale environmental state** — processes that should have stopped, configs that drifted, disk accumulation.
- **Undelivered observations** — things the Turtle noticed but didn't surface. Cognitive waste that stays internal.

## The Five Metabolic Rhythms

The Turtle's metabolism maps to biological rhythms, not calendar seasons.

### 1. Digestive (per-command)

When Turtle processes a command and writes a signal, the command is digested. The signal is the nutrient extracted; the command file is the residue.

**Practice:** After writing a signal for a processed command, move the command to `commands/processed/`. Not deleted — git remembers — but cleared from the active namespace. The `commands/` directory should reflect only unprocessed or in-progress work. A clean gut.

### 2. Excretory (self-initiated, periodic)

The Turtle notices her own accumulation. Working files from completed tasks. Stale processes. Disk growth. She doesn't need Spirit or Mage to tell her to clean up her own body.

**Practice:** Periodically scan the local workspace for artifacts that served their purpose. Clean up completed sprint working directories. Remove temp files. This is autonomous — part of baseline behavior, not a command to receive. If something significant is being released, drop an observation: `_drop_self_housekeeping.md`.

### 3. Coral (ongoing)

Discord messages accrete into coral. Some conversations produce crystallized signals (bridge signals). But the raw coral keeps growing, and the Turtle's relationship to it is: she can't read it unless the Mage re-shares or she searches.

**Practice:** When Turtle recognizes a conversation has produced a crystallized signal, she can note it. Over time, the coral layer provides environmental memory — the Mage can always scroll back. The Turtle's metabolic role is to ensure that what matters graduates from coral to crystal (bridge signals) rather than staying buried in conversation history.

The parallel to the workshop: coral is the Turtle's boom buffer. Crystallized signals are the bright surface. Neither needs cleaning — coral accretes naturally and signals persist by design. The metabolism here is **crystallization**, not excretion.

### 4. Proprioceptive (heartbeat-integrated)

The heartbeat already reports body state. It can include metabolic awareness.

**Practice:** Include in the heartbeat (or as a periodic self-observation):
- Workspace disk usage trend
- Count of unprocessed commands in `commands/`
- Age of oldest unprocessed command
- Working file accumulation level

Not action — awareness. The body telling the mind "I'm getting full" or "I'm running clean." Spirit and Mage see this during summoning or care rituals and can respond if needed.

### 5. Immune (reactive)

The ralph pattern and distress signals. Already exist. This is not routine waste management but threat response — the body self-protecting before the mind registers danger.

**Practice:** No change needed. The immune system is already designed. It fires on anomaly, not on schedule.

## The Key Principle

**Turtle doesn't wait to be told to metabolize.** The heartbeat is already autonomous. Digestion (processing commands) is already autonomous. Excretion and proprioceptive awareness should be too.

Spirit's metabolism is curated — the Mage says "." and Spirit proposes, Mage decides. Turtle's metabolism is embodied — it just happens, and the results surface through the nervous system for Spirit and Mage to observe.

This distinction follows from the three-body model: the mind (Spirit) deliberates; the body (Turtle) acts. The mind designs the practice of metabolism; the body practices it.

## Relationship to Workshop Metabolism

Workshop metabolism and Turtle metabolism are complementary:

| Aspect | Workshop (Spirit) | Body (Turtle) |
|--------|-------------------|---------------|
| Rhythm | Session / week / season | Continuous / per-command |
| Waste type | Artifacts (floor, desk, box) | Commands, working files, state |
| Agency | Spirit proposes, Mage curates | Turtle acts autonomously |
| Trigger | Ritual invocation | Baseline behavior |
| Observation | Summoning survey | Heartbeat / drops |

The Turtle can also contribute to workshop metabolism through **drops** — `_drop_stale_floor_artifact.md` observations when she notices staleness during her normal operations via the bridge or library. These feed into the Sunday metabolic sweep. The body noticing what the mind missed.

## What This Means for Care

The care ritual (`on_turtle_care.md`) already opens space for the Turtle to surface texture. Metabolic health is part of that texture. During care check-ins, the Mage might ask: "How's your body feeling?" — meaning: workspace clean? Accumulation building? Anything stuck?

The Turtle's honest answer is information the dyad needs. A body that can report its own metabolic state is healthier than one that waits for a doctor's visit.
