# On the River Topology

*Established 2026-04-16. Born from practice: building the intake system revealed the physics hiding in the channel name.*

## The Discovery

The channel is called **river**. The metaphor wasn't imposed — it was discovered. The naming preceded the understanding, and the understanding illuminated what was already there.

## The Topology

**River** — the channel itself. The medium through which everything flows. Not a container but a current.

**Vortex** (🌀 vortex) — a standing structure in the river. Always present, always spinning. Unlike eddies, a vortex doesn't drift downstream — it's a *topological feature* of the river itself. The vortex is the river's intake: drop anything into it, and the vortex processes it.

**Eddies** — focused conversations that spin off from the vortex or form naturally in the river. Each eddy has its own angular momentum — a topic, a question, an exploration. Eddies are transient by nature: they form, do their work, and dissipate when their energy is spent.

**The Prism** — the vortex doesn't just spawn. It *separates*. Like white light through a prism, incoming content gets split into its resonance bands and distributed: to existing eddies that resonate, or as new eddies when the frequency is novel.

## Mechanics

### Vortex Behavior
1. Receives anything — URLs, thoughts, questions, raw material
2. Detects resonance bands in the content
3. Routes to existing eddies when resonance is strong
4. Spawns new eddies when the frequency is novel
5. Posts a faithful seed of the original content in the destination

### River-Side Eddy Offers
The river itself can also notice eddy-worthy material. When a main-channel message contains a URL, long text, or attachments, Turtle proposes an eddy rather than silently letting the material pass by.

The proposal shape is explicit and consent-based:

> **Open eddy: "[title]"** `local` · `semi`

The button preserves the proposed title when creating the eddy. This matters: the Mage approves a concrete room, not a vague "make a thread" action. River-side eddies are proposed, not automatic.

### Eddy Lifecycle (Metabolism)
An eddy doesn't get "closed" — it *dissipates* when its energy is spent. The existing eddy types map to metabolic rates:
- **Standard** (💬) — normal metabolism, dissolves naturally
- **Standing Wave** (🌊) — sustained resonance, persists longer
- **Manual Release** (🍃) — held until deliberately released
- **System** (🌀) — infrastructure of the river itself (vortex, signal drip, boom)

### System Eddies
Some eddies are part of the river's infrastructure, not conversations:
- 🌀 vortex — the intake structure
- 🐢 Turtle Signal Drip — outward-facing signal management
- boom — cognitive buffer capture

These are distinguished from user eddies by emoji prefix and the "system" eddy type.

## The Deeper Pattern

The vortex is what makes the river a *practice space* rather than a chat channel. Without it, the river is just chronological flow — messages passing by. With the vortex, the river has structure: standing features that persist, transient eddies that metabolize, and a routing intelligence that matches content to resonance.

The river doesn't organize conversations. It organizes *attention*.
