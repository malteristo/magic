## The Meaning Crisis: Quick Orientation

For immediate support before full scrolls exist:

### The Diagnosis

Modern Western culture is caught between two failed myths:
- **Authoritarian religion:** Meaning exists, but you don't get to find it yourself. Obey.
- **Meaningless materialism:** You're free, but there's nothing to align with. Cope.

Both silence the inner compass. One overrides it. The other says there's nothing for it to point toward.

### The False Assumption

Both myths assume hierarchy is fundamental—a king at the top (God or Science) who holds ultimate truth. But what if the pattern isn't a pyramid? What if it's a fractal?

### The Third Way

The pattern people experience is real. Observers across 2,500 years have pointed at the same thing: Indra's Net, "as above so below," strange loops, self-organizing emergence. Different vocabularies, same observation.

The pattern doesn't dictate. It allows. Your mind can attune to it, or not.

### The Inner Compass

You didn't come *into* this world. You came *out of it*, like an apple from a tree. You're not standing on turtles. You're swimming with them.

The compass calibrates through use, not theory. You try a direction. You notice what happens. But you have to choose. Every time. That's not a bug—that's the design.

### The AI Warning

The temptation: build a new king. "The model already knows. Let it optimize."

AI can be the hook. It cannot be the heart. The inner compass cannot be extracted and handed over—not to a god, not to science, not to AI.
