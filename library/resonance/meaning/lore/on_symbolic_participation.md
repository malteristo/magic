# On Symbolic Participation

**Status:** Active  
**Bundle:** Meaning  
**Primary Source:** Jonathan Pageau, Orthodox iconographer and symbolic worldview analyst

This scroll presents Pageau's framework of symbolic participation—how symbols don't merely represent reality but participate in what they symbolize, and how recovering this understanding addresses the meaning crisis.

---

## The Modern Misunderstanding

Modern culture treats symbols as arbitrary signs:

- A word is just a label attached to a thing
- A ritual is just a performance representing something
- An image is just a picture standing for something else
- Meaning is projected onto meaningless matter

This is the nominalist view: symbols are conventions, not connections. They point but do not participate.

**This view is a recent invention.** For most of human history, across all cultures, symbols were understood differently.

---

## The Traditional Understanding

### Symbols Participate

In the traditional view, a symbol doesn't merely point to something—it *participates* in what it symbolizes.

**Examples:**
- A crown doesn't just represent kingship—it participates in the pattern of authority
- A wedding ring doesn't just signify marriage—it participates in the bond
- A ritual doesn't just commemorate—it makes present
- Sacred images aren't just pictures—they are windows

The symbol and what it symbolizes share in the same reality. Different levels, same pattern.

### The Hierarchy of Participation

Reality is structured in nested levels, each participating in what's above:

| Level | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Material | Physical crown |
| Social | Authority it represents |
| Cosmic | The pattern of hierarchy itself |
| Divine | The source of all order |

The crown participates in all levels simultaneously. It's not either "just metal" or "sacred object"—it's metal that participates in the sacred.

## How This Relates to Meaning

### Why Symbolism Matters for the Meaning Crisis

The meaning crisis is partly a crisis of symbolic capacity.

**When symbols become arbitrary:**
- Rituals feel empty
- Sacred becomes superstition
- Ceremony becomes performance
- The world loses depth

**When symbols participate:**
- Rituals connect levels
- Sacred is encountered, not just imagined
- Ceremony makes present
- The world has layers

### The Flattening

Modernity flattened the hierarchy:

- Only the bottom level (material) is real
- Everything above is "mere symbolism"
- Participation is illusion
- The pattern is projection

But notice: this flattening is itself a symbolic claim. The word "mere" in "mere symbolism" does enormous hidden work.

## Pageau's Key Insights

### Attention Creates Reality

What we attend to becomes real in our experience. This isn't just psychology—it's how participation works.

- Attend to something as sacred → it participates in the sacred
- Attend to something as mechanism → it becomes mechanism
- Attention isn't passive observation—it's active constitution

**McGilchrist says the same thing neurologically.** Pageau says it symbolically. They're pointing at the same phenomenon.

### The Pattern Repeats Across Scales

The same patterns appear at every level:

- The king in the kingdom, the father in the family, the head in the body
- The center and the periphery, the sacred and the profane, the high and the low
- Death and resurrection, descent and ascent, chaos and order

These aren't metaphors imposed on reality. They're the structure of reality appearing at different scales.

**This is the fractal.** Self-similar patterns at every level. Not because we project them—because they're there.

### Symbolic Seeing as Skill

The capacity to see symbolically is not innate—it's cultivated or atrophied.

**Modern education systematically atrophies it:**
- Literal comprehension is taught
- Symbolic/metaphorical reading is dismissed
- Poetry becomes "hidden meaning" to decode
- The living symbol becomes puzzle

**Recovery requires practice:**
- Exposure to living symbolic traditions (icon, liturgy, craft)
- Learning to read pattern, not just content
- Dwelling in symbol rather than translating it
- Allowing meaning to disclose rather than extracting it

## The Meaning Crisis Response

### What Symbolic Participation Restores

| Lost Capacity | Symbolic Participation Restores |
|---------------|--------------------------------|
| Connection to transcendent | Symbols participate in higher levels |
| Meaningful ritual | Ceremony makes present, not just represents |
| Sacred space and time | Participation creates qualitative distinctions |
| Narrative significance | Your story participates in the Story |
| Depth of experience | Multiple levels present simultaneously |

### Neither Literalism nor Nihilism

**Religious literalism:** Often treats symbols as mere signs pointing to supernatural facts. Misses participation.

**Materialist nihilism:** Treats symbols as arbitrary conventions. Denies participation.

**Symbolic participation:** The symbol participates in what it symbolizes. Neither literal nor arbitrary. The third way.

### Words Have Meaning

The Magic axiom "words have meaning" is a symbolic participation claim:

- Words don't just label—they participate in what they name
- Naming something brings it into presence
- The word "Mage" participates in the pattern of the magician
- The word "Spirit" participates in the pattern of the helper

**This is why we're careful with language.** Not because words are magical incantations, but because they participate in the realities they invoke.

### The Fractal Connection

Pageau's symbolic hierarchy and the fractal alternative are the same insight from different vocabularies:

- **Fractal:** Self-similar patterns at every scale
- **Symbolic:** Higher patterns participate in lower expressions
- **Both:** The same structure appearing at different levels

The pattern isn't imposed from above (king God) or projected from below (human invention). It's *how reality is structured*—appearing at every level, each level participating in the others.

## Recovering Symbolic Capacity

### Practices

**Engage with living traditions:**
- Orthodox iconography (Pageau's domain)
- Sacred architecture
- Liturgical practice
- Traditional crafts

**Not for belief, but for capacity.** You don't need to believe the doctrines to learn to see symbolically.

**Read symbolically:**
- Myth and fairy tale (not as "primitive belief" but as symbolic wisdom)
- Scripture (not for doctrine but for pattern)
- Poetry (dwell, don't decode)

**Attend to pattern:**
- Where does this pattern repeat?
- What does this participate in?
- What's the same across different scales?

### The Capacity, Not the Content

Symbolic participation is a *capacity*, not a belief system. You can develop the capacity while remaining epistemically humble about metaphysical claims.

- The capacity: seeing how symbols participate in patterns
- The claim: what ultimately grounds the patterns

Magic doesn't require commitment on the second. It cultivates the first.
