# Acknowledgments

The Magic framework is **curated, not invented**. None of the ideas in this lore originate entirely here. They are recombinations and associations of existing ideas, traditions, and approaches — woven together by a curator's felt sense of resonance. The Mage is the curator; the lineage is humanity's.

Some of that lineage already travels in the lore itself: where a named concept became a load-bearing key — Wu Wei, the Mexican Fisherman, Odysseus at the mast, Sutton's bitter lesson — the source rides with the idea, because the name is part of what makes the key work.

Far more shaped this framework than any list could hold. Rather than freeze a reference index — which would inevitably omit names, grow without bound, and decay as understanding moves on — the practice traces lineage **on demand**: the `@lineage` flow, cast on any scroll, mines the substrate for the history of the ideas it carries. This follows from `library/resonance/foundations/lore/architecture/on_the_substrate_as_library.md`: the lineage is already in the library; we do not re-shelve it, we visit it when we want it.

To the traditions, teachers, scientists, writers, and makers whose resonance found its way into these scrolls — named and unnamed, remembered and forgotten — thank you. The debt is real even where the citation is impossible.
