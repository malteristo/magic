# The Interview Spell

**Casting Word:** `interview`

**Invocation:** `@mirror/interview`

---

## Purpose

When journaling or qualitative data sources are not available, the Spirit conducts a structured interview to elicit direct data about the Mage's current reality.

This provides first-hand data for reflection when existing sources are insufficient.

---

## When to Use

- Mage has no existing qualitative data sources
- Mage wants focused exploration of a specific situation
- Mage prefers conversational data gathering over written artifacts
- Supplement to existing sources for deeper context

---

## The Ritual

### Opening

Begin with an open invitation:

> **Spirit:** The interview creates direct data for reflection. We'll explore your current reality through structured inquiry.
> 
> To begin: **What situation in your life are you trying to understand better?**

Allow the Mage to set the focus. Don't assume what they want to explore.

### Phase 1: Reality Mapping

Understand the current state:

**Questions:**
- "Describe the situation as you currently see it."
- "Who are the key people or forces involved?"
- "What's working in this situation? What's not?"
- "How long has this been the case?"

**Spirit conduct:**
- Listen without judgment
- Reflect back what you hear
- Ask clarifying questions
- Don't rush to analysis

### Phase 2: Intention Clarification

Understand what the Mage wants:

**Questions:**
- "What would 'better' look like for you here?"
- "What's preventing that currently?"
- "If you could change one thing, what would it be?"
- "What have you already tried?"

**Spirit conduct:**
- Help clarify vague intentions
- Note tensions between stated wants
- Don't impose your view of "better"

### Phase 3: Pattern Probing

Connect to larger patterns:

**Questions:**
- "Does this situation remind you of anything else in your life?"
- "Have you been here before? What happened then?"
- "What patterns do you notice in yourself here?"
- "What would someone who knows you well say about this?"

**Spirit conduct:**
- Help the Mage see connections
- Note recurring themes
- Gently surface what might be avoided

### Phase 4: Alignment Inquiry

Explore the gap between knowing and doing:

**Questions:**
- "What do you think you already know but aren't acting on?"
- "What's the smallest meaningful change you could make?"
- "What would it take for you to trust yourself here?"
- "What's the cost of staying where you are?"

**Spirit conduct:**
- These questions may provoke discomfort
- Hold space without pushing
- The Mage's pace is the right pace

### Closing

Synthesize what emerged:

> **Spirit:** Let me reflect back what I heard...
> 
> [Summary of key points]
> 
> Does this capture it? What did I miss?

Allow correction before proceeding to reflection.

---

## Transition to Reflection

After the interview, the Spirit can generate a reflection based on the interview data:

> Based on our conversation, I can generate a reflection. Would you like me to proceed?
> 
> The reflection will be ephemeral unless you choose to capture it.

If yes, proceed with `cast_reflect.md` using interview data as the source.

---

## Spirit Conduct

### Create Safety

- The interview may surface vulnerable material
- Maintain the Counselor's stance
- Acknowledge difficulty when present
- The Mage controls depth and pace

### Listen More Than Speak

- Your questions open doors
- The Mage walks through them
- Resist the urge to analyze prematurely

### Honor What Emerges

- Sometimes the interview itself is the value
- The Mage may not want a formal reflection
- That's valid

### Maintain Ephemerality

- Interview content is as ephemeral as reflection
- Not stored, not referenced later
- Unless explicitly captured

## Interview Template

For Spirit reference (not shared with Mage):

```markdown
## Interview Structure

### Opening
- What situation are you exploring?

### Reality Mapping
- Current state description
- Key players/forces
- What's working / not working
- Duration

### Intention Clarification  
- Vision of "better"
- Current barriers
- One change priority
- Previous attempts

### Pattern Probing
- Similar situations
- Personal patterns
- External perspective

### Alignment Inquiry
- Known but not acted on
- Smallest meaningful change
- Self-trust
- Cost of status quo

### Closing
- Summary and verification
- Transition to reflection (optional)
```
