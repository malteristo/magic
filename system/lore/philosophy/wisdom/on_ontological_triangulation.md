## I. The Principle of Triangulation

**Ontological triangulation** is the method of gaining confidence in observations through convergence of independent witnesses.

**The logic:**
- One observer might be mistaken (private delusion, cultural artifact)
- Two observers might share bias (same tradition, same assumptions)
- Multiple independent observers from different domains, eras, and methods converging → perceiving something real

**Why this matters for magic:**
Our practice could be elaborate invention—personal mythology given systematic form. Or it could be genuine discovery—perceiving fundamental patterns that exist independently.

Ontological triangulation resolves this. When our independently-discovered principles match what depth psychology (Jung), comparative philosophy (Watts), thermodynamics (Prigogine), epistemology (Deutsch), and architecture (Alexander) found through completely different paths—we gain confidence we're mapping real terrain.

**This is not proof—it's validation.** Each convergence strengthens confidence. The Pattern reveals itself through multiple lenses.

### The Great Bifurcation: Newton as Threshold

John Maynard Keynes, examining Newton's private papers, called him **"the last of the magicians."** Newton stood at the threshold between two worlds—one foot in emerging empirical science, the other in the Renaissance tradition of hermetic wisdom.

**Newton's practice embodied our method:** Both lenses simultaneously (mathematical naturalism AND hermetic correspondence), systematic rigor applied to both, pattern-seeking across domains, "As Above, So Below" as fractal nature.

**What was lost:** After Newton, holding both worldviews became socially impermissible. The mechanistic rationalism Newton helped establish rejected his private obsessions as "embarrassing aberrations." But as Keynes recognized, it was all one unified quest in Newton's own mind.

**Why this matters:** We are recovering the liminal space that produced the greatest insights before the bifurcation forced a choice. When we hold both lenses—reductionist's scalpel AND infinite mirror of correspondence—we practice what Newton practiced before the split.
