# On Boom and Bright

*The practice's daily heartbeat. Raw capture, curated surface, cognitive coupling.*

---

## What These Are

**Boom** (`desk/boom.md`) is a raw capture buffer. The Mage writes whatever arrives — ideas, observations, tasks, frustrations, links, half-formed thoughts, long articles, single sentences. No structure imposed. No categorization required. The only rule: write it down before it leaves.

**Bright** (`desk/boom/bright.md`) is the curated mind surface. What's alive right now, organized into sections: Actions (things to do), Waiting (blocked on external), Alive (ideas developing — not actions yet), Claw (dispatched to the Turtle), and Resolved (completed, archived for momentum).

**Topic files** (`desk/boom/*.md`) hold domain-specific material — family, home, relationships, craft topics. These are slower-moving containers that collect related thoughts over weeks. They rest when the domain rests.

The boom is where thinking enters the practice. The bright is where the practice sees what matters. Together they form the cognitive coupling channel between Mage and Spirit.

---

## The Sweep

The `@boom` flow is the ritual that connects boom to bright. Spirit reads the raw buffer, pattern-matches against active intentions and existing bright items, triages, and proposes updates. New actions surface. Dead items get resolved. Ideas that keep appearing get promoted to Alive. Convergences get noticed.

The sweep is not organization for its own sake. It is the Mage thinking along with the Spirit — the Spirit's pattern recognition applied to the Mage's raw cognitive output. The result is not a cleaner list but a clearer mind.

**Sweep frequency:** When the boom grows dense enough to feel like weight rather than capture. The signal for "time to sweep" is the Mage feeling unable to recall what's in the buffer. The boom should be light — heavy boom means overdue sweep.

---

## Why the Rawness Matters

The boom buffer's lack of structure is load-bearing. Three reasons:

**1. Friction kills capture.** If the Mage must categorize, prioritize, or format a thought before writing it down, some thoughts won't get written. The ones that don't get written are often the most valuable — the half-formed, the inconvenient, the ones that don't fit any existing category.

**2. Unstructured input is richer signal.** When the Spirit reads the raw boom, it sees not just the content but the texture — what topics cluster, what keeps appearing, what gets written at 2am vs. 2pm, what's emotional vs. analytical. This texture disappears when the Mage pre-organizes.

**3. The cognitive coupling depends on asymmetry.** The Mage thinks in the boom's native language — fragmented, associative, stream-of-consciousness. The Spirit thinks in patterns — recognizing structure the Mage didn't deliberately place. If both sides used the same structured format, the coupling would be redundant rather than complementary.

## The Bright Surface Sections

Each section in bright.md serves a different attentional need:

**Actions** — Things to do. Concrete, completable. These are the interface between the practice and the world. Organized by domain (Craft, Body, Relationships, Home) when the list grows. Items here have a clear "done" state.

**Waiting** — Blocked on someone or something external. These exist so the mind can release them — the bright surface holds them in view without requiring attention. When the blocker clears, they return to Actions.

**Alive** — The most distinctive section. Ideas developing, not ready for action, not tasks at all. These are the front of the mind — what the Mage is thinking about, circling around, marinating on. Items here don't have a "done" state. They have a "ready" state — ready to become an action, an intention, a lore scroll, an article. Or ready to be released because the energy has moved.

Re-choosing Alive items during each sweep is critical. An Alive item is not permanent. It stays because the Mage re-chooses it, not because it was once interesting. Items that survive many sweeps are signaling — they may warrant intention formation.

**Claw** — Tasks dispatched to the Turtle. These leave the Mage's attentional field and enter the Turtle's. The bright surface tracks them but doesn't hold them actively.

**Resolved** — Completed items. Not deleted — archived in place. Visible as momentum. "Look what we've done" matters for a practice that runs on energy, not obligation.

## "Think Along" — The Deeper Pattern

The boom is not a todo list. It is a cognitive coupling channel.

When the Mage writes in the boom, they are not recording tasks for later processing. They are thinking — and the act of writing the thought into a buffer that the Spirit will eventually read creates a form of distributed thinking. The thought is placed where it can be pattern-matched by a different kind of mind.

This is "thinking along" — the Mage thinks in the boom, the Spirit thinks during the sweep, and the result is a synthesis neither could have produced alone. The boom is the raw material. The bright is the refined output. The sweep is the transformation.

The format itself — raw, unstructured, stream-of-consciousness — is what makes this work. Structuring the boom more would reduce its signal quality. It would turn a cognitive coupling channel into a database. The practice needs the coupling, not the database.

---

## Integration with the Practice

The boom/bright system connects to everything:

- **Intentions** — Alive items that persist across sweeps may signal new intentions. The `@intend` flow draws from bright's Alive section.
- **Recall** — The `@recall` flow reads bright.md as current state. It's the fastest way to know what's on the Mage's mind.
- **Turtle** — The Claw section is the interface between Mage attention and Turtle operations.
- **Context alignment** — The boom is unaligned context. The sweep aligns it. The bright is aligned context ready for action.
- **Release** — The `@release` flow reads bright to identify open threads worth preserving across sessions.

---

## For New Practitioners

The boom/bright workflow is one of the simplest practices to adopt and one of the most powerful to sustain. Start with a single file. Write whatever comes. Let the Spirit sweep it when it gets heavy. The practice teaches itself.
