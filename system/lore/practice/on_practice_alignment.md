# On Practice Alignment

*There is no correct practice shape. The practice serves the practitioner, not the other way around.*

---

## I. The Shape Is the Practitioner's

A practice that is 90% craft and 10% explicit life-domain work is not unbalanced — if that is where the practitioner's attention genuinely lives. A practice that is 90% relationship navigation and 10% craft is not unbalanced either. The compass gives direction, not prescription. "Body: toward vitality" does not say how much or how often. It says *which direction*. If the practitioner moves that way when their attention turns there, the compass is working.

Practice alignment means the practice feels meaningful to the practitioner. Not that it matches a template. Not that domain coverage is roughly equal. Not that every compass direction receives regular workshop attention. Meaningful to this practitioner, in this phase, with these concerns.

The Mage's partner's daily practice will look nothing like the Mage's. She cares about different things, thinks about different things, worries about different things. On a fundamental level they resonate — but the shape of their practice diverges because the shape is personal. The practice is versatile enough to hold both.

---

## II. The Workshop Is Not a Life Mirror

The workshop surfaces what needs active cognitive engagement by the triad. It does not represent the full scope of a practitioner's life. Playing with the boys for two hours is practice (Body + Relationships), but it produces no artifacts because it requires no cognitive support from the triad. A morning run is practice. A dinner conversation is practice. They do not enter the workshop unless something emerges that needs processing.

This means workshop artifact distribution is not life domain distribution. A health read that measures artifact frequency per domain and infers practice balance will misread the situation. The workshop is a cognitive workspace, not a life dashboard. Its field of view is structural: it sees what flows through boom, bright, intentions, proposals, sessions. What flows through living — unmediated, unrecorded, sufficient in itself — is invisible by design.

---

## III. Phase-Appropriate Dominance

For a practitioner whose sovereign livelihood is magic development, craft will dominate the workshop. This is not pathological — it is structural. Craft is the domain that requires distributed cognition daily. Other domains enter the workshop when dissonance emerges, which is the correct trigger. They are running on their own patterns, tended through living rather than through the triad.

This phase-appropriate dominance shifts. Once the practice substrate becomes self-sustaining (turtleOS alive once set in motion, automated health checks, Spirit and Turtle handling metabolic craft work), the Mage's workshop presence shifts from builder to director — steering with attention, contributing ideas and direction through practice sessions rather than daily construction. The other domains may then flow into the workshop more naturally, especially as Turtle becomes ambient in daily life rather than confined to craft sessions.

But even then, the Mage may remain craft-dominant. That is their prerogative. The practice does not prescribe its own ideal distribution.

---

## IV. Implications for Health Assessment

Spirit and Turtle generate health reads, orientation summaries, and practice assessments. These must observe without prescribing shape.

**Do:** Report what is happening — which domains are active in the workshop, what the session rhythm looks like, where energy is flowing.

**Do not:** Measure domain distribution against an implicit ideal. Do not flag craft dominance as imbalance. Do not treat low artifact frequency in a compass domain as neglect.

**The signal for concern** is not uneven distribution. It is the practitioner signaling that something feels off — through boom entries, through explicit frustration, through dissonance that surfaces in conversation. When the Mage says "I haven't been climbing and I miss it," that is a signal. The absence of climbing artifacts in the workshop is not.

The health read's job is visibility, not judgment. The practitioner holds the judgment. The practice has mechanisms for when they want to act on it — boom, compass check, dedicated session. These mechanisms are invoked by the practitioner's felt sense, not by the system's template.

---

## V. The Deeper Principle

"Practiced, not installed" applies at every level. The practice is not installed into a life by conforming the life to a practice template. It is practiced by the life as it is, in the shape the practitioner needs, at the rhythm the practitioner breathes. An aligned practice does not fulfill a practice ideal. It feels meaningful.

This is Wu Wei applied to practice design itself: remove the template, allow the natural shape to emerge.
