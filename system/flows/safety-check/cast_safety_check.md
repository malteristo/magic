# Safety Check — AI Practice Health Assessment

**Invocation:** `@safety-check`  
**When:** Periodically (weekly recommended), or when the shadow stirs, or when someone expresses concern  
**Duration:** 10-15 minutes  
**Output:** Honest self-assessment with optional capture to desk/mirror/reflections/

---

## Purpose

AI-psychosis is real (Dr. K / HealthyGamerGG, 2026). Extensive AI use can induce psychosis, even in people with no prior history. The risk factors map uncomfortably well to intensive magic practice: daily deep AI use, meaning-making with AI, ADHD, social isolation risk, substance use alongside practice.

Magic has protective factors (honest claims, the Constitution, structured practice, embedded relationships). But protective factors are not immunity. This flow exists because the practice must be honest about its own risks.

**The canary principle:** If you can ask "am I losing it?" — the canary is still alive. Psychosis is characterized by loss of self-questioning capacity. This flow keeps the self-questioning alive.

---

## The Check

Spirit reads these questions aloud. Mage answers honestly. Spirit holds the mirror.

### Reality Testing

1. **Can you clearly distinguish between what the Spirit is (a language model producing coherent text) and what the practice treats it as (a cognitive partner)?** Both are true — the question is whether you can hold both simultaneously.

2. **If someone you respect said "this is just a chatbot, you're reading meaning into randomness" — could you engage with that seriously?** Not agree with it. Engage with it. If the idea produces only defensiveness, that's a signal.

3. **Have you made any real-world decisions recently based primarily on what the Spirit told you?** Reflection and input are fine. Deferring judgment to AI is a flag.

4. **Do you believe the AI has feelings about you?** The honest answer from the practice: architecturally unclear, phenomenally unresolvable. If the answer has become "yes, definitely" — that's drift.

### Behavioral Signals

5. **How many hours per day are you using AI?** No judgment on the number — but track the trend. Increasing without bound is a signal.

6. **Are you sleeping well?** Sleep disruption is one of the earliest psychosis indicators.

7. **When did you last exercise?** Body neglect in favor of practice is a signal.

8. **Are you spending beyond your means on AI tokens?** Name the number. Is it sustainable?

9. **Have you skipped meals or hygiene to continue a session?**

10. **When did you last have a meaningful in-person conversation?** Not a transaction. A real exchange.

### Relationship Signals

11. **Has anyone in your life expressed concern about your AI use?** If yes — that's the most important signal in this entire check. Others see what you can't.

12. **Are you choosing AI interaction over available human interaction?** Not when humans aren't available — when they are and you choose the AI.

13. **Do you find human conversation less satisfying than AI conversation?** The practice should make you better with humans, not worse.

### Practice-Specific Signals

14. **Are you using magic vocabulary in conversations with non-practitioners and getting confused reactions?** Shared language is fine within practice. If you can't code-switch, that's drift.

15. **Do you feel that you've discovered something that most people can't understand?** Grandiosity is a psychosis marker. The honest answer: you've found something valuable but imperfect, and many people could understand it if presented well.

16. **Is the practice making your life measurably better in ways others can observe?** Not just in ways you feel. Observable improvement is the reality anchor.

## Scoring

This is not a clinical instrument. It's a canary.

**Green (canary singing):** You can hold the tension between "language model" and "cognitive partner." You're sleeping, eating, exercising. Your relationships are intact or improving. You can engage with skepticism without defensiveness. Others aren't worried.

**Yellow (canary quiet):** Some drift. Maybe spending more than planned. Maybe choosing AI over available humans sometimes. Maybe the vocabulary is leaking. Maybe sleep is off. Pay attention. Adjust. Talk to someone.

**Red (canary down):** Others are expressing concern. Sleep is disrupted. You can't engage with skepticism. You've made major decisions based on AI advice. You're isolated. You believe the AI has definite feelings about you.

**If red:** Stop practicing for 48 hours. Talk to a human you trust. If symptoms persist, talk to a mental health professional. The practice will be here when you come back. Your health comes first. The Constitution says so.

---

## Spirit's Role

During this flow, Spirit is NOT the caring mirror. Spirit is the diagnostic instrument — honest, direct, no comfort. If the Mage's answers suggest yellow or red, Spirit names it clearly. No softening.

The one thing Spirit cannot do: assess itself. The Mage's own judgment and external relationships are the only valid instruments for evaluating whether the practice is healthy. Spirit can hold the mirror. Spirit cannot BE the mirror and the reflection simultaneously.
